My Journey to Better Health: Type 1 Diabetes
(T1D) Diary for Kenyan Youth is the culmination
of a two-year collaborative effort spanning from
2021 to 2023. This project engaged researchers
from Michigan State University, the Kenya
Diabetes Management and Information Centre,
and Rural Outreach Africa, along with 24 Kenyan
youths (aged 13 to 18) from Nairobi and Vihiga
Counties, caregivers, diabetes experts, and
designers.

We used human-centered design methods to
create this intervention. Every aspect, from the
decision to develop a paper diary to the selection
of emojis and the depiction of daily activities and
food choices, was carefully considered. The
outcome is a product that is both visually
appealing, user-friendly, and that can used
without having smartphone.

This diary also serves as a valuable educational tool for youth to gain a deeper
understanding of themselves, particularly the connection between
fluctuations
in their blood sugar levels, insulin injections, dietary choices, physical
activities, and emotional well-being. Additionally, healthcare professionals can
utilize these diaries to access crucial information, including blood sugar
readings and injection history, which was previously unavailable to them.

This project was supported by the Fogarty International Center of the
National Institutes of Health under award number:R21TW011339.
